About

sMITe (or smite, or Smite, or SMITE!, or anything, really…) has been around MIT for almost 20 years. While not a perennial powerhouse, we have a history of being competitive in both the region and the nation. Most of all, though, we have a history of working hard together and trying to push each other to both be our best and have the most fun.

New Players

What is ultimate?

Ultimate, short for ultimate frisbee, is a team sport played with a flying disc. Players on a team pass the disc between them and score points by catching it in the opposing end-zone.

What’s the difference between sMITe and byte?

sMITe is our A team, and byte is our B team. sMITe is more competitive and committed; byte is more focused on development and a little more relaxed on commitment. sMITe has uneven playing time, Byte has even playing time.

How often are practices?

We have practices 2-3 times a week. Take a look at our Schedule for details about when and where practices are.

Do you guys go to competitions?

Yup! We compete against other colleges in the USA Ultimate College Series. Some competitions are local, but we've also competed in Maine, Connecticut, Texas, Rhode Island, Florida, and Virginia! During the fall semester, we typically go to 4 tournaments, and in the spring, we have tournaments pretty much every month.

Do I need experience to join?

If you’ve never played Ultimate before — fear not! Many of us hadn’t either until we came to MIT. We’ll teach you everything you need to know to play the game and have lots of fun. If you have played before, all the better! We can teach you new skills and help you take your game (and our team) to the next level.
